Create a reproducible local research workflow that produces 100 verified Japan recruitment/headhunter contact records in the current project folder.

## Scope

Collect 100 contact records for Japan-based recruitment businesses across all industries.

The dataset uses a mixed model:

- Company-level contact details are preferred.
- Individual consultant details are allowed only when published on the company's own public website or another clearly public business directory.
- Every record must include at least one source URL for the contact detail.
- Every company must have a verification path showing why it belongs in a recruitment/headhunter dataset.

## Field Rules

- `record_id`: stable slug generated from company name plus source URL.
- `contact_name`: blank for company-level contacts.
- `title`: blank unless a named person's title appears on the public source page.
- `email`: blank when none is public. Do not infer email patterns.
- `phone`: normalized Japan phone string where possible. Keep original visible value when normalization is uncertain.
- `contact_form_url`: absolute URL.
- `source_url`: URL where contact detail was found.
- `mhlw_source_url`: URL or documented MHLW portal/search URL used for verification.
- `license_number`: MHLW permission/notification number when found, for example `13-„É¶-000000`.
- `license_type`: `śúČśĖôŤĀ∑ś•≠ÁīĻšĽčšļčś•≠`, `ÁĄ°śĖôŤĀ∑ś•≠ÁīĻšĽčšļčś•≠`, or blank if not MHLW verified.
- `evidence_keywords`: semicolon-separated keyword list.
- `verification_status`: one of `mhlw_verified`, `association_verified`, `business_keyword_verified`, `needs_manual_review`.
- `confidence`: `high`, `medium`, or `low`.
- `source_accessed_at`: ISO 8601 timestamp with timezone.
- `notes`: short human-readable caveat, if any.

## Confidence Rules

- `high`: MHLW verified or recognized association/certification verified, and contact detail appears on official company website.
- `medium`: business keyword verified on official company website, and contact detail appears on official company website.
- `low`: contact detail appears on public third-party page, or classification is ambiguous. Low-confidence rows do not count toward the required 100 unless the user explicitly approves.

## Data Quality Rules

- The final 100 rows must be unique by normalized `(company_name, email, phone, contact_form_url)`.
- At least 80 rows should be `high` confidence unless official sources are unavailable for enough targets.
- No row may have an empty `source_url`.
- No row may use a private person source unless the page is clearly public business information.
- Raw reads must be saved so each processed row can be audited later.
- The QA report must list counts by `verification_status`, `confidence`, `classification`, and missing-field category.

## Compliance Boundaries

Japan PPC APPI guidance treats email addresses and already-public personal information as potentially protected personal information depending on context. This project therefore minimizes personal data:

- Prefer company-level contact channels over individual personal contact channels.
- Store only business contact data required for source-traceable research.
- Do not infer private email addresses.
- Do not collect home addresses, personal social media handles, private phone numbers, or non-business notes.
- Do not bypass access controls, login gates, CAPTCHAs, robots-protected private areas, or paid databases.
- Keep source URLs and access timestamps for traceability.

Reference official PPC pages:

- https://www.ppc.go.jp/personalinfo/faq/APPI_QA/
- https://www.ppc.go.jp/personalinfo/legal/guidelines_tsusoku/

## Dokobot Usage Requirements

Use Dokobot for web search and page reads where a normal static fetch is insufficient or where rendered pages need a browser:

- Check `dokobot --help`, `dokobot search --help`, and `dokobot read --help` before execution.
- Prefer `dokobot read --local` when the local bridge/browser is available.
- Save each Dokobot output to `data/raw/dokobot/`.
- For MHLW and other official pages, capture the source URL and read timestamp.
- If a site requires manual interaction to expose results, document the action in `data/raw/manual_observations.md`; do not bypass protections.

## Acceptance Criteria

The project is ready when:

1. `data/processed/japan_headhunters_contacts.csv` has exactly 100 accepted rows.
2. The same records exist in `data/processed/japan_headhunters_contacts.jsonl`.
3. Every row has `source_url` and at least one contact channel.
4. Every row has a non-empty `verification_status`.
5. `qa_report.md` reports zero critical validation failures.
6. A spot check of 10 random rows confirms source URLs support the contact details and classification.
7. The implementation can be rerun without duplicating rows.

## Out Of Scope

- Building a full all-Japan recruitment company database.
- Sending outreach email or making phone calls.
- Enriching with private LinkedIn, paid databases, or inferred personal email patterns.
- Continuous monitoring.
- CRM upload.
- Legal advice. The workflow enforces conservative data handling but does not replace counsel.
